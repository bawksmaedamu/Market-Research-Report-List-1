<p><ul><li>Concentrate</li><li>Non-Concentrate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Juice market types primarily include concentrate and non-concentrate segments. Concentrate juice is made by removing water from the juice, resulting in a denser product that can be reconstituted by adding water before consumption. This approach often enhances shelf life and reduces transportation costs. Non-concentrate juice, on the other hand, retains its original water content and typically offers a fresher taste, appealing to health-conscious consumers. Both types cater to different preferences and demands within the beverage market.</p></p>
