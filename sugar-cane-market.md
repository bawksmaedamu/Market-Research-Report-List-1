<p><p>The sugar cane market includes various types, primarily categorized into chewing canes and syrup canes. Chewing canes are cultivated for direct consumption, prized for their sweetness and fibrous texture, appealing to consumers as a natural snack. In contrast, syrup canes are grown specifically for juice extraction, used to produce sugar, molasses, and syrups. Each type serves distinct consumer preferences and industries, impacting cultivation practices, regional distributions, and economic contributions within the broader agricultural sector.</p></p>
