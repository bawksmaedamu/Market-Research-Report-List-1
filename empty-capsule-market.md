<p><p>The empty capsule market is primarily categorized into two types: gelatin-based capsules and vegetarian-based capsules. Gelatin-based capsules are made from animal by-products and are widely used in pharmaceuticals and dietary supplements due to their excellent bioavailability and ease of manufacturing. In contrast, vegetarian-based capsules, often made from hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC), cater to the growing demand for plant-based alternatives, appealing to vegans and those with dietary restrictions. Both types serve critical roles in drug delivery and nutraceutical applications.</p></p>
