<p><p>The medical grade stainless steel market primarily includes 304, 316, and other specialized alloys. Grade 304 is widely used for its excellent corrosion resistance and formability, making it suitable for surgical instruments and equipment. Grade 316 offers superior resistance to chlorides and is ideal for implants and devices exposed to bodily fluids. Other grades include 420 and 440, which are used for specific applications requiring different mechanical properties. The choice of grade depends on the application’s rigorous demands and environmental conditions.</p></p>
